## What is an Algorithm?

1936 Church-Turing Thesis

An algorithm:

1. Is set out in terms of a finite number of exact instructions expressed in a finite number of symbols.
2. If carried out without error, will produce the desired result in a finite number of steps.
3. Can be carried out by a human being unaided by any machinery except paper and pencil.
4. Demands no insight, intuition, or ingenuity on the part of the human being carrying out the method.

## Computational Models

- It is possible to become extremely precise about the computational model you are using: what counts as one "step"?
- In this class, we will generally treat a "line of code" as "one step" unless specified otherwise
- Exceptions: loops, etc
- Whole field based on making different assumptions about computational model: quantum, nondeterministic, time travel, highly parallel, etc
- *Complexity classes* are defined in terms of underlying computational models: P vs. NP
